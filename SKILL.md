# Coffee Chat Prep (Before / During / After)

Take a founder peer 1:1 (warm intro, 30-90 min, dual purpose: build the relationship + tactical learning) through a complete workflow.

> **Mindset first (read before any tactic):** no one is obligated to help you. Every invitation and ask must be polite and carry genuine gratitude. This attitude comes before everything below — it's the foundation the whole skill rests on; the tactics only work on top of it.
>
> **Core belief:** a high-ROI founder coffee chat isn't about how many lessons you extract — it's about making the other person **proactively think of you six months later**. The whole flow serves that.
>
> **Companions:**
> - Suggested question bank -> `question-bank.md` (pick by node type / purpose)
> - Deep product-demo / user-feedback capture -> use a separate user-interview skill (this skill's after-phase stops at "meeting wrap-up").

## Scope

✅ Warm-intro founder peer, 30-90 min 1:1, dual purpose (relationship + learning), a candidate in your contacts
❌ Sales / investor pitch, cold first meeting, internal meetings, recruiter/candidate interviews, pure social hangout

Not a fit -> report why, don't force it.

# 🅾 Requesting a chat in a community (etiquette)

Before any of the phases below — if you're signing up for a chat by commenting on a community post (someone offering coffee chats), basic etiquette sharply raises your hit rate. The host is scanning many comments and deciding who's worth a slot.

1. **Comment from a real, identifiable account** — not an anonymous or handle-only profile.
2. **Have a filled-out profile** — a short bio / self-intro and a real profile photo. People partly decide whether to talk to you from this alone.
3. **In the comment itself, always include three lines:**
   - **Who you are** — how to address you + your status (student / founder / employed)
   - **What you're working on right now**
   - **What you'd like to talk about**

> These three lines are the baseline courtesy of signing up — they let the host instantly judge fit and know what to prep. A bare "interested!" from an empty account gets skipped.

# 🅰 Prepare (Before)

## Step 1 — Gather variables (ask for what's missing, don't re-ask what's given)
1. Name / company / role / stage
2. Venue / time / **duration** (45 vs 90 min = totally different structure)
3. Intro path (who introduced, how warm)
4. Their recent public signal (IPO / news / launch / article / funding)
5. Your real motive for this meeting (program prep? broadcast? pure curiosity? they asked?)
6. Your default give (what resource / network / data can you offer?)

## Step 2 — Node type (decides what this meeting aims for)

| Node | What you get | Best fit | Timing |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Broadcast** | Spread to a larger network through them (program batch channels, etc.) | 1-2 yrs ahead, Series A, active network | only valuable once they're mature |
| **Playbook** | First-hand experience from someone who just walked the path you want | just finished that path, memory freshest | **earlier the better — memory decays** |
| **Ally** | Long-term peer, similar stage, compatible direction | stage + domain fit, complementary not competing | show up repeatedly, build slowly |

> Founder filter (small sample, don't hard-code): a founder who's just entered a top program (elite accelerator / VC inside track) sees their need for outside help drop sharply -> lean Playbook node, don't expect a long-term Ally. But don't hard-code the label — some program alums still become genuine long-term allies, so read actual chemistry over the heuristic.

## Step 3 — Takeaway goal (agent lists candidates -> user decides)

> ⚠️ The goal is not set by the agent. Based on node type + their stage, the agent lists **2-3 primary takeaway candidates** and asks the user to choose via an interactive prompt; only the user's pick goes into the prep (pick "Other" if none fit).

**Primary candidate routing:**

| Their stage | Primary orientation |
|---|---|
| Peer founder (same layer, on the path) | Relationship: "X proactively thinks of me in 6 months", seed reciprocity |
| Top-program alum (accelerator inside / Series B+) | Playbook: "get an actionable playbook + leave a next-round opening" |

❌ Wrong at any stage: "get N lessons" extraction framing.

**Bonus (optional, at most one):** a single insight, not a full playbook. Its function is to push you from extraction -> listening mode.
**Inner purpose (private, you carry it in):** a non-transactional inner frame, e.g. "I'm here to see who this founder really is, find a peer mirror." It leaks through tone / follow-up depth / how you handle silence.

## Step 4 — Talking outline (timeline 20 / 55 / 25)

- **Segment A open (~20%)**: you give a one-line positioning + frame the talk (state the ask first, if any) -> hand to them. Don't go deep yet, build chemistry.
- **Segment B your agenda (~55%)**: 3-5 questions, **ranked by importance**, marked "may not ask all — read chemistry", flag **risky ones**.
  -> pick from `question-bank.md` by node type / motive, each with 1-2 sub-clarifiers.
  - Ranking: actionable for you > they're willing to share > unique to this person (don't ask what's publicly searchable)
- **Segment C give (~25%, no less than 10 min)**: open with "What are you most trying to solve right now that you haven't cracked? (not just work)" -> the default offer is only a backup.

### Short vs long CTA (bring extra ammo for long talks)
High-chemistry meetings often run long on their own — well past the booked time.
- **Short (30-45 min)**: standard follow-up + 24h deliver
- **Long (90 min+, unlocks only after the first natural break)**: have 1-2 long-horizon strategic questions / proposals ready, e.g. "If you were pushing X into the US next year, how would you design the first 30 days?"
- Don't push to extend, but when it extends naturally, **have something to throw**.

## Step 5 — (Conditional) Collab proposal — 4 principles
> Only when there's real collaboration potential (esp. dev-tool / infra founders). Whether the proposal's *shape* fits matters more than demo strength.
1. **Match their current distribution / user gap** — design it as a light integration for them with value that lands directly
2. **Low-stakes framing** — lead with "let's just try it, might not work" to lower the temperature; deeper commitment unlocks later
3. **First-give order** — for a 2-layer proposal: do L1 first (you do the work, zero effort from them), then L2
4. **Concrete anchor on the spot** — drop a specific goal in the first 30 seconds to align with their motive, so the follow-up has a hook

## Step 6 — (Conditional) Introducer relationship management — pre
> Warm intro only. Don't stack multiple asks on the same introducer; report results back after; treat them when you're in their city; think about what you can give the introducer in return.

## 🚫 Hard rules — don't ask (first meeting)
- Don't ask for an intro (any intro)
- Don't ask for their customer list
- Don't ask for specific metrics (revenue / pricing / numbers)

## Output: prep note (markdown)
Title + meta (time / venue / duration / hard stop) -> node call -> takeaway (user's pick) -> talking outline (A/B/C + short/long CTA) -> cheat sheet to bring in.

# 🅱 Live (During)

## Conversation beat sheet (script, glanceable on phone)
> Written in second person, not a teaching tone. Time ratio = Segment A/B/C.

- **First ~20%**: small talk to warm up -> your one-line positioning of your company + frame the talk ("I'm working on X, so I especially wanted to talk to you") -> hand over, let them cover their pivot / recent state. **Feed them material** so they have a chance to ask you for detail.
- **Middle ~55%**: once they've finished introing -> you follow up (Segment B questions, ranked). Mid-section, throw a **peer-mode ball** (an unfiltered misstep / judgment of your own) -> watch whether they reciprocate to decide how deep to go.
- **Last ~25%**: open-ended first — "what are you most trying to solve and haven't?" -> only map a give to what they say; throw the default only if they say nothing.
- **Final 5 min**: restrain yourself, don't raise the next step. With 30 sec left and they haven't raised it -> give a low-commit opening: "I'm in X through Y, ping me anytime to catch up or for an intro."

## Observation cues (5) — live cheat sheet
1. **Emotional energy**: which part is alive (real conviction) vs rehearsed (protecting / hasn't thought it through)
2. **How they frame external entities (VC / partner / competitor)**: "they get me" (insider, survivorship bias) / "hard to crack" (raw, useful) -> affects how you weight their advice
3. **Deflection points**: a topic skated past with technique = something they're protecting; note it, don't dig, approach gently next time
4. **Spark moment**: when you update them on your business, which line makes them lean in / ask back / want detail = a natural future-collaboration touchpoint -> carry into the debrief
5. **Reciprocity signal (most critical)**: any unprompted offer (intro / advice / resource) = the strongest signal for a layer upgrade

(Add a 6th custom cue for special sensitivities, e.g. stealth mode.)

## Live chemistry check (4 signals)
> **Core: throw the ball first, then observe.** You chat normally and tactically choose when to switch to peer mode. The signal measures the other side's *initiative direction / depth* under a peer-level exchange.
1. They ask you for detail — probing 4-5 layers vs a polite single line then dropping it
2. They share something not said publicly (**main engine**) — you switch to peer mode and go unfiltered first -> do they reciprocate or not
3. They make "next time / collaboration" concrete — giving a date / logistics vs a vague "sometime maybe"
4. They naturally raise the next meeting (you **restrain** from raising it) — they bring it up vs just swapping contacts

| Hits | Reading | Action |
|---|---|---|
| 3-4 | real chemistry -> Layer 2 candidate | Overinvest: 24h extra + meet again + substance back |
| 2 | program/batch warmth != chemistry | Light-touch: deliver + ping in ~1 month |
| 0-1 | one-off | polite 24h follow-up, done, set aside |

## Live discipline
- Not all questions get asked (read chemistry, don't chase the list)
- If you read defensive, stop (risk question hits a defense -> change topic)
- **Don't push the give if the spark isn't there**: when no spark shows, stop giving — the more you talk, the more it looks like you're convincing them they need you

# 🅲 Follow-up (After, within 24h)

> Posture: **they walk away with more >> you walk away with more.** Most founders go to a meeting to "learn"; the right way is the reverse — the other person walks away with something -> triggers "this person's got substance" -> raises broadcast odds.

## 24h deliver (send within 24h of meeting)
Two forms:
- **(a) Honor a promise**: what they asked for -> deliver in 24h (3 profiles / a 1-page data doc)
- **(b) Proactive throw**: a need they mentioned -> fill it unprompted (intro / candidate)

Concretely contains (a)(b)(c):
- (a) deliverable mapped to their pain point (swap the default offer if it doesn't fit)
- (b) something you brought but didn't finish (data sample / context)
- (c) one unfinished question = round-2 hook

(For a priority node, sketch a follow-up email frame: Subject -> opening 1-2 lines -> (a)(b)(c) -> closing + next ping rhythm)

## Lightweight debrief
- Write the spark moment + reciprocity signal into your contact note for this person
- One line of next ping rhythm (what timing / what pretext to reconnect, e.g. "after their IPO" / "when my Q3 data is out")
- If they're a tracked contact -> update their layer status

## Layer upgrade tracking (5 signals, observe 2-4 weeks after)
1. They **proactively** intro you to a third party
2. They mention you publicly (post tag / podcast / event intro)
3. When you hit a difficulty, they **proactively** check in / help
4. They share unfiltered real metrics / thoughts (not the PR version)
5. They remember what you said last time, follow up next time

-> **None of these happened = still Layer 1.5**, no matter how many times you've met. Upgrades don't come from the meeting itself, they come from these 5.

## (Conditional) Introducer report-back + product-feedback handoff
- warm intro -> report results to the introducer within 72h
- if this meeting had a product demo / user feedback to dig into -> hand off to a separate user-interview skill, don't mix it in here

## 🚫 Anti-pattern table (7, hard-earned)

| # | Anti-pattern | Phase |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Too purpose-driven / asking for too much info (treating them as an information source) | 🅰 |
| 2 | Every segment packed, no room for chemistry (leave a 20-30% buffer) | 🅰 |
| 3 | Throwing the default offer in the give segment (without first asking "what do you most want to solve") | 🅰/🅱 |
| 4 | Takeaway written as "get N lessons" | 🅰 |
| 5 | Questions with no ranking, forcing a live decision | 🅰 |
| 6 | Observation cues written as abstract principles instead of an actionable cheat sheet | 🅱 |
| 7 | Pushing the give when it isn't landing (throwing without reading the spark) | 🅱 |
