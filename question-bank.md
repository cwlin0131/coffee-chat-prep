# Coffee Chat Question Bank

> Companion to the `coffee-chat-prep` skill. When building Segment B, pick 3-5 questions here by **node type + your motive**, rank them, and attach 1-2 sub-clarifiers each.
> Keep it growing: after each chat, append the new questions that worked to the right topic.
> Ranking principle: actionable for you > they're willing to share > unique to this person (don't ask what's publicly searchable).

Picking guide (node type -> priority topics):
- **Playbook node** -> ② fundraising/applications / ① GTM / ③ positioning (get first-hand path experience)
- **Ally node** -> ⑦ peer-mirror / ① GTM / ⑤ product (build relationship + mutual learning)
- **Broadcast node** -> ⑦ peer-mirror mainly (build impression > extract), few tactical questions

---

## ① GTM / early user acquisition
*Fits: almost any node; required when your motive includes "learn GTM"*

- How did you find your first users? Which channels worked, which died outright?
  - sub: what was the first *repeatable* channel? How did you discover it?
- Which "type" did your early customers come from? How did you reach them?
- From zero to the first retention signal, how long? What was the turning point?
- (dev tool / infra) How does GitHub acquisition work — competitor user scraping / open-core, etc.?
- What's the most useless GTM move you tried that everyone says you should do?

## ② Fundraising / applications playbook (accelerators / programs)
*Fits: Playbook node, program alums; top priority while you're running an application*

- Which application questions changed the most? Original vs revised — what was the difference?
  - sub: which old answer missed the mark? Why?
- What do partners actually care about? Most common pitfalls? What do non-US founders most underestimate?
- What exactly did the rejection letter say? Was it actionable feedback or abstract direction?
- How fast do reference calls come? How do you prep customer / industry refs in advance?
- This round or wait for the next? (asking the person who did it is more honest than asking a partner)
- Between submit and interview, how to prep? Dos/don'ts of the founder video?

## ③ Positioning / narrative
*Fits: Playbook node, anyone struggling with positioning*

- How did you converge on your positioning? Which framing did you switch from/to, and what triggered it?
- Which *words* are investors allergic to? Which framing did you have to retire?
- Vertical-specific vs horizontal — how did you choose?
- How many versions did your one-liner go through? Why did the final one work?

## ④ Founder / team / solo
*Fits: peer founder, Ally node*

- Solo founder (or a co-founder left) — how did you push through?
- Non-US founder + local background — how do you answer "why not SF / why you"?
- Team size? Do you believe in "small team of 2-5 for fast AI iteration"? How do you split work?
- Which hire did you get right / wrong?

## ⑤ Product / retention
*Fits: Ally node, same-domain peer*

- Where was the hardest stretch for retention? How did you handle it?
- Which features did you decide *not* to build? How did you decide?
- Which feature got a user reaction most different from what you expected?

## ⑥ Operations (bicoastal / visa / time zones / cross-shore)
*Fits: bicoastal peers or those entering the US*

- Bicoastal in practice: flight cadence, time zones, how the team holds up?
- What pitfalls did you hit with visas / company setup?
- How far ahead is one market vs another on information? How do you exploit the lag?

## ⑦ Peer-mirror / meta (not extraction — for building the relationship)
*Fits: all nodes, esp. Broadcast/Ally; use to switch to listening mode, seed reciprocity*

- Your pivot trajectory over the years + why? (warm-up opener, listen for conviction)
- Jumping from investing / big-co / e-commerce to building your own — what was the biggest unlearn?
- What's keeping you up at night right now? (not just work)
- If you started over, the earliest decision you'd change?

## Long-version CTA questions (unlock only at 90 min+)
*Throw only when the conversation extends naturally; strategic / long-horizon:*

- Suppose I'm pushing [product / X] into the US next year — how would you design the first 30 days?
- If you were me, where would you put 80% of your energy at this stage?
- Is there a "let's just try it, might not work" level small collaboration we could start? (connects to the collab-proposal 4 principles)

---

## Risk questions (may go defensive — advance by feel, stop if you read defensive)
- Their protected secret sauce (specific acquisition channel / hook / numbers)
- Fundraising details (valuation / terms)
- Competitor / former-employer sensitive topics

> Risk-question rule: list them but mark "⚠️ risk question", remind the user to read the room, don't dig hard.
